It is easy to think of the kitchen as the stomach or digestive system of the house, though this comparison is tenuous. A contrast: the kitchen and dining room are the heart of the house, the center of activity and socialization. The hallways and corridors of a house are its veins, providing circulation coursing throughout its frame. A staircase bears more than a passing resemblance both physically and symbolically to a spine. The windows serve much the same purpose as eyes, and anyone who has rounded a bend or long fdrive and come suddenly face to face with a tall, dark manor will tell you that it is difficult to shake the impression that the house, through its lightless windows, is a creature capable of vision and intelligence.

The kitchen is supposed to feel like the warmest place in the house, and when we first moved in it was. Cooking together. Eating together. But then there were too many late-night arguments, too many dishes started piling up. Heh, the dishes are still here. I used to blame it on the house starting to change, on it making my parents act like people they aren't. But I think it was always that way. They were just better at hiding it before.

Mom turned into a husk after Dad disappeared. With how much they argued before he disappeared, you would think she would've been happy. But they needed each other. For better or worse. I remember the last time I talked to her. She asked me if I wanted something to eat. I said yes. She started cooking in the kitchen. I was in my room down the hall, scared to leave for no reason I could think of. I only left when I smelt the burning. When I walked into the kitchen, she wasn't there anymore. I can still smell the burning. Yeah, I could use some fresh air. - X
