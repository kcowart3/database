My old bedroom. My moon light is still on. Living in this house, the dark scared me. It still scares me now, to be honest. I guess I should be happy that the electricity still works in here. Though, I don't know how much comfort the light is to me anymore. We're taught that light means purity, safety, god, and all other good things. But what happens when the light can't save you anymore? No matter how many switches you have on, the boogeyman is still standing at the end of the hallway. Not a pile of clothes or a coat on a hanger. No tricks needed. Your worst nightmare just standing there, his presence too absolute for you to write it off as a nightmare. This house was my boogeyman. It still is.

Nowadays, when I get lucky enough to have a friend let me bum on their couch or find a motel that pities me enough to let me lay out on one of their beds, sometimes I'll wake up here. In my old bed, in my old room. I pray you never know what it's like to run from your own past. To feel stuck in your own labyrinthine childhood.- X
