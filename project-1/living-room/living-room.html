The living room. Often the dominant space of a house at ground level, as well as typically the center of activity in a well-populated home, the living room is very much the heart of the house. While a human heart circulates blood to oxygenate the body's extremities, the living room circulates people, activity, communication. It is the room most often to be found "beating," as active and vivacious as its name would imply. The comparison is only strengthened when we consider also the living room is most commonly the room to contain the fireplace, making it additionally a locus of actual, physical heat.

If the living room is the heart of this place, then it certainly is proud to be that. Pictures to display its living nature, like trophies. Though, all of the pictures are black. And no, I don't mean the frames are empty. I mean the pictures are black. The longer I look at them, the more I feel like there's an image stored inside. Trapped, but watching me from beyond the void. Somehow that's made all the more disconcerting by the fact that there's one that isn't darkened. a picture of me, Mom, and Dad. I pick it up and look at it for a moment, temporarily transported into the frozen moment held in the frame. I remember the excitement of moving into a new place. I had only known one dinky place for a large percentage of my short existence, so somewhere fresh that was bigger and newer and nicer was precisely what I wanted. And precisely where my parents wanted me. I put the picture back down, perfectly back where I wanted. It felt wrong to disturb this place's bizarre sense of order. This place isn't just the heart, but for me it's the first circle. I had plenty more to descend. - X
